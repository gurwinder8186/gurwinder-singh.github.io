Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori health model by Sir Mason Durie that highlights four key pillars: physical, spiritual, family/social, and mental health. Balance across these areas is essential for holistic health.

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

1 hour of cardio for overall fitness.
Playing with kids in the park to stay active and strengthen family bonds.
Teaching my daughter to ride a bike, which includes running alongside her, helps improve my health while also bonding and sharing valuable life skills.
Doing push-ups during study breaks to stay energized and active.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Eating together daily as a family.
Playing with my kids to strengthen family bonds.
Regularly speaking to my father and mother overseas for their wellbeing.
Going to the temple together as a family.
Asking my daughter daily how her day at school was.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Doing breathing exercises during study breaks.
Listening to music for relaxation and mental clarity.
Reading articles related to personal interests or learning.
Practicing intermittent fasting to enhance mental clarity.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Meditating in remembrance of God early in the morning.
Doing morning and evening prayers.
Listening to religious hymns for spiritual connection and peace.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Going to parks 2 to 3 times a week for fresh air and nature connection.
Spending time outdoors under the open sky to enjoys sunshine.
Planting small plants, such as mint and coriander, for home and kitchen use.
