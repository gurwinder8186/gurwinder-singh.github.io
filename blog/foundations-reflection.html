What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Through the Human Skills learning, I’ve discovered a few key insights about myself and how my ego might play a role. I tend to focus heavily on my own work, preferring to work independently and paying less attention to seeking or offering help or interacting with others. This could be driven by a subtle desire to prove my capabilities without needing external assistance. I've also realized that I often neglect my health and mindfulness, pushing through long study sessions without taking proper breaks, which may reflect a drive to achieve results quickly, even at the expense of my well-being. Lastly, I’ve noticed that I sometimes struggle with distractions while studying on the computer, often getting sidetracked by videos or content that is unrelated to studies that pulls me away from focused work. This balance between wanting to stay productive and giving in to these distractions reflects a challenge in managing both focus and ego-driven goals.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy, and self-awareness play vital roles in both learning and programming. Values like integrity and discipline guide ethical decision-making and a commitment to high-quality work. Empathy allows programmers to understand user needs, collaborate effectively, and design solutions that are user-friendly and impactful. Self-awareness helps individuals recognize their strengths and areas for improvement, manage stress, and maintain a growth mindset. Together, these qualities foster a more thoughtful, ethical, and collaborative approach to problem-solving and personal development in programming.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

What surprised me the most about the Human Skills learning is how much it influences programming, something I didn’t initially expect. At first, I thought programming was purely about problem-solving and technical skills, with no real connection to human skills. However, I quickly realized that applying mindfulness, self-awareness, taking breaks, and even incorporating focused exercises has a significant impact on productivity and the quality of my work. These skills help improve focus, reduce stress, and boost overall performance, making them essential for success in programming.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning for me were listening and looping, as it was difficult to give someone my full attention consistently—something I wasn’t used to doing in my daily routine. Additionally, practicing mindfulness through meditation proved to be both effective and challenging, as it wasn’t something I had incorporated into my routine before, making it harder to adjust to.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

The main reason Dev Academy focuses so much on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp is because, as explained in the Foundations, employers often value strong human skills more than technical ones. While technical skills can be taught relatively quickly, human skills—like communication, empathy, and mindfulness—are harder to develop but essential for workplace productivity and a positive environment. These skills not only improve collaboration and problem-solving in teams but also enhance focus and health, making the learning process more effective and sustainable in the long run.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don’t think the time spent learning Human Skills was a waste at all. In fact, it was a great decision. It didn’t take away from learning programming but rather enhanced it by making the sessions more focused and attentive. Human skills are essential for success, especially in the workplace, as they complement technical abilities and improve productivity. I’m thankful that these skills were taught, as they are just as important for employment and professional growth as programming itself.
