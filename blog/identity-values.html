Identity/Value

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I made the ethical decision to become a baptized Sikh (5th largest religion originated from north India), choosing to live by principles such as avoiding greed, lust, ego, anger, and attachment. This decision involved reflecting on the values of humility, self-control, and integrity, and was driven by my belief that these principles lead to a meaningful life free from negative behaviors like comparison, plunders and backstabbing. While it is challenging, I believe this path brings true fulfillment and peace, aligning me with a higher purpose. Looking back, I am proud of this choice, as it has made my life better and helped me stay grounded and at peace.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My culture and the people around me, especially my parents and siblings, have greatly influenced my values and identity, helping me become a more mature, caring, and respectful person. In our culture, it is highly valued to love, respect, and help those in need without expecting anything in return. I continue to work towards embodying these values for a more fulfilling and purposeful life.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths, such as having clear goals and avoiding diversions, will help me stay focused on my studies. My willingness to work hard supports spending intense hours on learning, and my openness to asking for help from facilitators and fellow students ensures I actively seek guidance and feedback when needed. These traits will be crucial in supporting my learning journey.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Some of my limitations include spending too much time trying to solve problems on my own, which can be exhausting, not taking proper breaks or using short focused sessions, and staying away from study for extended periods, which affects my concentration and ability to grasp complex topics. Additionally, being overly concerned with new gadgets and technology can sometimes distract me and take time away from my studies. These habits can slow down my learning journey and reduce my effectiveness.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

While working as a fiber technician installing broadband, which involved hard, physically demanding tasks, I noticed some coworkers were not putting in the same effort as I did. To address this, I planned ahead and divided the work equally so everyone could contribute to their full capabilities, which led to significant progress. Reflecting on this, I now realize the importance of planning and will apply this by reviewing tasks at the beginning of the week and scheduling them with proper time allocation to achieve better results.
