Problem Solving

Blocked on a simple problem:

what was the problem?

I couldn't figure out the solution for JavaScript kata's task "5-builtin-methods" to capitalize the first letter in each word of a sentence and return it as a string.

what problem-solving techniques did you use?

I tried applying different methods like split(), map(), toUpperCase(), and join(), googled for solutions, and used console.log() to check the output step by step. Even though it took me some time, In the end, I figured out the correct solution.

How did you feel throughout the process?

At first, I felt quite interested and excited as I was challenging my knowledge. However, after spending a while without making much progress, I started feeling stressed and stuck, as if I had fallen into a rabbit hole.

what did you learn?

Whenever we get stuck on something, we should return to it with a fresh mind. We shouldn't spend too long trying to solve a problem on our own. Instead, it's better to seek help rather than wasting hours and making no progress.

Elegantly solved a problem:

what was the problem?

I was trying to solve the problem of removing the word "buzz" from a sentence, but I had to ensure it was removed in a case-insensitive manner. The challenge was not only filtering out the word but also handling different capitalizations of "buzz."

what problem-solving techniques did you use?

I first thought through the steps in my mind and jotted down some pseudocode on a notepad. Then, I remembered that I had worked on a similar program before, where I used string manipulation functions like split() and toLowerCase(). I decided to apply a similar approach here by splitting the sentence, filtering out the word "buzz," and using toLowerCase() to handle case insensitivity.

how did you feel throughout the process?

At first, I felt tense because I wasn't making much progress. However, once I remembered that I had done a similar program before, ideas started coming to mind, and I felt more confident about solving the problem.

what did you learn?

I learned that reflecting on past solutions can be incredibly helpful when tackling similar problems. It's important to remember where to look for solutions when you've already solved something similar. I also realized that planning ahead is crucial when approaching challenging tasks.

Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

Pseudocode: I'm confident with pseudocode and using it as well. Planning and organizing thoughts before coding leads to clearer solutions.
Trying Something: I'm okay with trying new approaches, but I prefer to do so when I have spare time.
Rubber Ducky Method: I have friends with coding backgrounds to discuss problems with, so I haven't found the rubber ducky method necessary.
Reading Error Messages: I'm comfortable interpreting error messages and identifying relevant information and main errors from them.
Console.logging: I'm proficient with console.log() for checking outputs and understanding code behavior before finalizing solutions.
Googling: I'm good at Googling and know which key websites to use for finding solutions and examples.
Asking Your Peers for Help: I'm open to seeking help from peers and plan to use this support more as I progress towards the bootcamp.
Asking Coaches for Help: I'm confident in reaching out to facilitators when I need quick solutions or when peer-level help isn't sufficient.
Improving Your Process with Reflection: I find reflecting on past experiences very helpful for problem-solving and skill improvement.

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time

There was a time when I was stuck on a JavaScript kata task, "5-builtin-methods," which involved capitalizing the first letter of each word. It was around midnight, and I was eager to finish the task before leaving. I decided not to reach out for help, thinking I should solve it on my own despite being stuck for a while.

What made me reluctant was the late hour and my desire to complete the task independently. I thought I should be able to resolve it without assistance, even though it was consuming a lot of time.

Next time, I plan to reach out for help sooner rather than spending excessive time struggling alone. Seeking assistance earlier can save time and allow me to focus on learning important concepts more effectively.
